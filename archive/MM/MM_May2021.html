Mental Health Awareness Week 2021

Next week is Mental Health Awareness Week (10th May - 16th May) in the UK and this year the theme they want us to focus on is nature. Research has shown that going for walks outside was one of our top coping strategies and 45% of us reported being in green spaces had been vital for our mental health. A place to start with unwanted thinking

This image really captures the place to start when you have an unwanted thought, try to set some distance from yourself and that thought. Many thoughts come up during the day and a lot of them may or may not be true, one exercise to practice whenever a thought comes up to make you feel bad is to set some distance. See if you can add a line to the thought such as "I am having the thought that..." or even "I notice the thought that..." just to give a bit of space between yourself and the thought. This can really help release the claws in some persistent negative thoughts that you might be struggling with.

Your mindfulness exercise
 
In every edition of the Mindfulness Monthly we have an exercise to get you centred and feeling refreshed.

Mindful Appreciation

Choose 3 things in your day that usually go unapreciated. These things can be objects or even people.

For example, the electricity that powers your kettle or the person who delivers your post.
Ask yourself:

How do these things work?

How do they benefit you?

Have you ever noticed the finer details of these items?

The point of this exercise is to give thanks and appreciate the seemingly insignificant things in life. The things that support us but rarely get a second thought.
